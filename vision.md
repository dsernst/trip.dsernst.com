# Vision

## What is Random Trip?

Random Trip is a trip coordination ritual for people who love each other but don't live near each other.

The core problem: life gets busy, and visits with family and old friends keep getting deprioritized. Nobody means for it to happen — it just does. Random Trip is the structure that makes it happen anyway.

## The one-line pitch

> For the people you keep meaning to see.

## The core idea

A group commits to a shared ritual: we're meeting up somewhere new, regularly. The destination is chosen for them — based on where everyone is, what everyone can afford, and what the group has loved in the past.

Nobody has to plan. Nobody has to be the person who picks. The hard parts are automated. All anyone has to do is join.

## Who it's for

**Primary audience: family, spread out.** Siblings, cousins, adult kids and parents who live in different cities or countries and want a recurring reason to see each other.

**Secondary audience: old friends.** College crews, former coworkers, people who drifted geographically but want to keep the ritual of staying in touch alive.

The common thread: people who already want to see each other, but need a structure to make it actually happen.

## What it does

1. **Invites a group** — any size, not locked to a specific number of people.
2. **Picks a destination** — based on individual locations, individual budgets, and accumulated group reviews. Nobody votes. Nobody debates.
3. **Tracks who's in** — anyone can opt out of any specific trip, at any time, no explanation needed. The ritual continues regardless.
4. **Collects post-trip reviews** — short notes (1–2 sentences) from each traveler that feed into the next pick. The selection gets smarter over time.

## The opt-out philosophy

The commitment is to the ritual, not to every trip. This is a feature, not a limitation — it removes the pressure that causes people to avoid committing in the first place. Life happens. The group keeps going.

## Budget & location

Everyone sets their own spending limit individually — no group negotiation, no awkwardness. Everyone shares roughly where they are. The destination is chosen with both in mind.

**Closer trips** are easier to coordinate and cheaper. Good for tight schedules or lower budgets.

**Farther trips** are more of an adventure — more travel time, more cost, potentially timezone changes. Both have a place in the rotation.

## What this is not

- Not a travel planning app. It doesn't book flights or hotels.
- Not a social network. Reviews are private to the group.
- Not AI-for-AI's-sake. The randomness and intelligence are means to an end: more face-to-face time with people who matter.

## The emotional core

Random Trip is a tool to maximize group face-to-face time. It makes coordination easier so the thing that actually matters — being together — happens more often.
