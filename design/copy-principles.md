# Copy Principles

These rules emerged from iterative refinement and capture decisions that are easy to undo without knowing the history. Follow them carefully.

---

## Voice

**Warm, but not sentimental.** The emotional payoff (staying close to people you love) is implied, not stated. Don't reach for it. Let it arrive on its own.

**Practical, not inspirational.** This is a tool. It makes hard things easier. Lead with that — the feeling follows from the utility.

**Confident, not hype-y.** No exclamation points. No superlatives. No "revolutionary" or "effortless" or "seamless." The product speaks for itself.

---

## Locked phrases

These have been deliberately chosen and should not be changed without good reason:

| Phrase | Where used | Why it's locked |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| "For the people you keep meaning to see." | Hero subtext, footer | Captures the core audience and the core problem in one line |
| "Random Trip handles the hard parts" | Hero subtext | Frames the product as a tool, not a lifestyle |
| "All you have to do is join." | Hero subtext | "Join" is warmer and lighter than "show up" — no pressure implied |
| "Closer trips are easier; farther ones are an adventure. Both have a place." | Budget section | Honest framing that doesn't oversell either option |
| "It gets better as you go." | Reviews section heading | Understated, true, earns its confidence |

## Rules

### Do not lock the group to a specific size
**Wrong:** "The four of us agree to a trip."
**Right:** "The group commits to meeting up somewhere new."

The product works for any group size. Never write copy that implies it's built for four people.

### Do not lead with the AI angle
**Wrong:** "An AI picks your destination." / "Powered by LLMs."
**Right:** "A destination gets picked for you."

The intelligence is a means to an end. The audience doesn't care how it works — they care that they don't have to do it themselves. Bury the mechanism; surface the outcome.

### "Join" not "show up"
"Show up" carries a faint obligation — like attendance is being taken. "Join" is an invitation. Use "join" when referring to participating in a trip.

### Reviews are private, not public
Reviews are short notes that feed back into the selection algorithm — they're for the group, not the internet. Do not frame them as Yelp reviews, star ratings, or anything public-facing. The word "review" is fine; just never imply an audience beyond the group itself.

### Opt-out is guilt-free by design
Never frame opting out as a problem, an exception, or something that needs to be earned. It's a core feature. The right framing: "anyone can sit out any round, for any reason, at any time. No guilt."

### Don't over-explain the algorithm
The destination is chosen based on locations, budgets, and past reviews. That's all the user needs to know. Do not go deeper than this in marketing copy. Save technical specifics for documentation.

## Tone by section

| Section | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Hero | Quiet confidence. Warm. States the problem and the solution without drama. |
| How it works | Clear and direct. Steps should feel light, not like a contract. |
| Budget & location | Honest and practical. Acknowledges real-life constraints. |
| Opt-out | Reassuring. Explicitly removes guilt. |
| Reviews | Understated optimism. The product gets smarter — no need to oversell it. |
| Footer | Minimal. Just the name and the tagline. |

## Things that were tried and rejected

- **"Not for the internet"** — Used to clarify that reviews are private. Confusing without context; readers assumed it was about Instagram or Yelp. Removed.
- **"Let someone else figure out where."** — Clever but slightly glib. Replaced with cleaner hero subtext.
- **"Four of us"** — Too specific. Implies the product only works for groups of four.
- **"All you have to do is show up."** — "Show up" felt pressuring and slightly anxious. Replaced with "join."
- **Leaning into AI/LLM language** — Early drafts framed the destination selection as an AI feature. Felt like AI-for-AI's-sake. Pulled back significantly.
